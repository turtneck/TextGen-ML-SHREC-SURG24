November 4 2016 Turkey Rounds Up Erdogans Political Opponents as Crackdown Widens Salahhatin Demirtas once hailed as the Kurdish Obama among the proKurdish opposition lawmakers held on Friday A Turkish court placed the two leaders of a major proKurdish opposition party under arrest on Friday in a dramatic widening of a political crackdown that followed Julys failed military coup that will raise concerns about the future of Turkeys parliamentary democracy Police detained the cochairs of the Peoples Democratic Party HDP Selahhatin Demirtas and Figen Yuksekdag early on Friday morning along with nine other lawmakers The measures against proKurdish officials more than three months after president Recep Tayyip Erdogan narrowly survived an attempt by part of the military to seize power on July 15 could also open a new season of conflict with armed Kurdish insurgents Hours after the arrests a car bombing reportedly killed at least eight people in the city of Diyarbakir the largest city in the Kurdishmajority southeast of Turkey
